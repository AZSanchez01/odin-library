Notes From the Underground
By: Fyodor Dostoevsky
| 256 pages
"Notes from Underground" is a novella by Fyodor Dostoevsky, published in 1864, that explores the psychology of an unnamed, alienated narrator, known as the Underground Man, living in 19th-century St. Petersburg. The book, presented as a fragmented and contradictory "confession," critiques rationalism, societal norms, and utopian ideals, delving into themes of existential despair, free will, and the complexities of human nature.
